Projects

Project 1
This online learning module is designed to teach second-grade students about food chains in an interactive and engaging way. Three digital media works I created are embedded in the project to effectively deliver the content. The digital video serves as a hook to attract and entertain students at the beginning of the lesson. It is a puppet show that engages young students in learning how energy is transferred from one living thing to another living thing by eating and being eaten in a food chain. The image is used as the main element to teach the key concepts with a vivid food chain example. The audio is aimed to provide a more detailed interpretation of the image to better support student learning.

Project 2
This board game is designed to reinforce players' math knowledge and skills related to U.S. money via game playing. The goal of this board game is for players to help Zoombinis to be the first one that collects $300. This game has a 6-sided dice, a spinner, three categories of question cards with their answer cards, a deck of function cards, coins and bills, a game board and the Zoombini game pieces. Players roll the dice to move their game pieces and collect the money shown in the space where their game pieces land. Then, players spin a spinner to determine what categories of cards they draw. If they draw a trivia card of a certain category, they need to answer the question correctly to earn extra money. If they draw a function card, they need to carry out the action described in the card.

Project 3
This is a group project for instructional design that I participated in. In this project, the team worked together to identify a performance problem of phonics teaching at the Mullanphy Botanical Elementary School via need assessment and gap analysis. After conducting the learner analysis, contextual analysis and task analysis, the team designed and developed a training program for the teachers to better implement phonics instruction.
