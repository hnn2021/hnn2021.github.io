Project Description

This board game is designed to reinforce players' math knowledge and skills related to U.S. money through gameplay. The goal of the board game is for players to help Zoombinis be the first to collect $300. The game includes a 6-sided dice, a spinner, three categories of question cards with their corresponding answer cards, a deck of function cards, coins and bills, a game board, and Zoombini game pieces.Players roll the dice to move their game pieces and collect the money shown on the space where their game pieces land. Then, they spin the spinner to determine which category of cards they draw. If they draw a trivia card from a specific category, they need to answer the question correctly to earn extra money. If they draw a function card, they must carry out the action described on the card. Overall, the game aims to strengthen players' math skills related to U.S. money while engaging them in an enjoyable and educational gaming experience.

Self-reflection

This learning experience has provided me with a deeper understanding of designing educational games. Incorporating high-order thinking skills into games and assessing those learning objectives in a game-focused way was both insightful and challenging throughout the process of designing this game. Looking back, if I were to redo this process now, I would make some changes to the game board layout and mechanics to enhance its appeal, engagement, and educational focus. Instead of simply labeling the amount of money on all spaces, I would transform some spaces into thematic stores, such as pet stores, candy stores, and bakery stores. By doing so, players would have a more immersive experience and feel connected to the game world. Moreover, I would introduce challenges in these spaces that align with the higher-order learning goals I want the players to achieve. These challenges could involve solving math problems related to buying items from the stores, managing finances, or making strategic decisions based on the available money. Incorporating these changes would not only make the game more enjoyable but also provide a meaningful context for players to apply their math knowledge and skills related to U.S. money. It would also promote critical thinking, decision-making, and problem-solving, all of which are valuable skills that players can benefit from beyond the game.Overall, this reflection has inspired me to continually strive for improving my game design skills and create more appealing and impactful learning experiences for players.
